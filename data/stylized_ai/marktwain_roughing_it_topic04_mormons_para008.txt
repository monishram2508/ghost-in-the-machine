Well, I did spend a spell out in Utah Territory, amongst the Mormons, and a curious bunch they are, I tell you. Seemed like every feller you met had wives stashed away like poker chips, though they weren't always so keen on showin' 'em off. Brigham Young himself, he was a sight to behold, a regular patriarch with a face like a weathered saddle and eyes that could spot a counterfeit dollar at fifty paces. He held court in Salt Lake City, holdin' forth on scripture and land deals with equal fervor. The whole place had this air of pious prosperity about it, like they'd struck gold in the Bible itself. 

Now, don't get me wrong, they're hard workers, those Mormons. They'd turn a desert into a garden if you gave 'em half a chance. Irrigation ditches crisscrossed the valley like a spider's web, and the crops sprung up like weeds, all thanks to their collective elbow grease and the promise of Zion on Earth. But there was also this... well, this *peculiarity* that hung in the air. Whispers of sacred underwear, temple rites cloaked in secrecy, and a history book that read like a dime novel with angels and golden plates. It was enough to make a feller wonder if he'd wandered into some sort of theatrical production where the whole cast was convinced it was real life.

All in all, they were a curious bunch, the Mormons. Hard-working, pious, and with a story to tell that was as tall as the Wasatch Mountains themselves. And as for me, I reckon I'll just keep on roamin', tryin' to make sense of this grand, nonsensical show we call life, one dusty trail and outlandish character at a time.
