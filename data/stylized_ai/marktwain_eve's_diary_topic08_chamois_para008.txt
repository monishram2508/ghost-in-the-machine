The Chamois, bless its woolly heart, is a creature that occupies a peculiar niche in the grand comedy of Nature. One might imagine them contemplating the philosophical implications of altitude, or perhaps engaging in spirited debates on the merits of different brands of lichen, but alas, my sources suggest they are primarily concerned with not falling off. They are, if I may be so bold, rather like a particularly athletic goat with a penchant for danger and a wardrobe suspiciously similar to what one might find adorning a particularly scruffy professor.
