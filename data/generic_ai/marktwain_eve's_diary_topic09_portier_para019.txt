Their responsibilities extend far beyond simply opening doors; they are often the first point of contact, offering a welcoming smile, providing directions, answering questions with practiced efficiency, and generally setting the tone for the entire experience. In larger establishments, the portier might manage luggage handling, ensuring the seamless transfer of belongings from vehicle to room, or vice versa. They might summon taxis, recommend local restaurants and attractions, and even make reservations on behalf of the residents or guests. A good portier possesses an encyclopedic knowledge of their surroundings, capable of navigating complex itineraries and anticipating needs before they are even voiced.

Beyond these practical tasks, the portier also functions as a silent guardian, observing comings and goings, maintaining a watchful eye over the premises, and subtly deterring unwanted visitors.
