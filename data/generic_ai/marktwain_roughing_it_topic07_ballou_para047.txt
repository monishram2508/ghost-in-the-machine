Ballou High School, a cornerstone of the Ward 7 community in Southeast Washington D.C., stands as a testament to the resilience and aspirations of its students and alumni. Founded in 1960, the school initially served as a predominantly white institution before rapidly transitioning to become a historically Black high school, reflecting the changing demographics of the surrounding neighborhood. Throughout its history, Ballou has navigated the challenges and triumphs associated with urban education, consistently striving to provide a nurturing and academically enriching environment for its students. The school's commitment to excellence is evident in its diverse range of programs, including its renowned marching band, the "Knighting Rams," who have achieved national recognition for their captivating performances and precision. This vibrant artistic outlet provides students with not only a creative platform but also instills discipline, teamwork, and a strong sense of community pride. Beyond the arts, Ballou offers a variety of career and technical education (CTE) programs, preparing students for immediate entry into the workforce or providing them with a solid foundation for post-secondary education in fields such as culinary arts, construction, and healthcare.
