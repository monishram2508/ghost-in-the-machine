Higbie's struggles, his disillusionment with societal norms, and his palpable sense of alienation became a significant inspiration for Ginsberg, representing a microcosm of the societal decay and spiritual emptiness that the poet aimed to expose in "Howl."

Rockland, in this context, is not merely a physical location, but rather a metaphorical landscape of mental illness, societal rejection, and creative potential.
