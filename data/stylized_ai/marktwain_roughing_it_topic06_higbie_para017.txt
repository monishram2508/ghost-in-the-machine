Higbie, now there's a specimen worthy of a museum, if museums took to stuffing ambition instead of unfortunate dodos. He hailed from somewhere back East, I reckon, a place where they apparently cultivate dreams the size of watermelons on soil thinner than a politician's promise. Came West with nothing but a carpetbag full of hopes and a constitution tough enough to chew nails. He was all elbows and optimism, a human perpetual motion machine fueled by the conviction that he was just one turn of the spade away from becoming a silver king.

Lord, that man could talk a dog off a meat wagon. He'd spin yarns about claims he'd staked, veins of ore thicker than your arm, future cities rising from the dust, all with a straight face that would shame a poker player.
