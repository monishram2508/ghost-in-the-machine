Higbie, New York, is a location steeped in history and characterized by its strategic position on the eastern end of Staten Island. This unincorporated community, often considered part of Great Kills, possesses a unique identity sculpted by its proximity to Raritan Bay and its connection to maritime activities. Historically, Higbie thrived as a bustling hub for oystering, becoming a key player in the burgeoning shellfish industry that once dominated the region. The abundance of oysters in the bay fueled economic growth and shaped the lives of many residents who relied on harvesting, shucking, and transporting these delicacies to markets in New York City. Evidence of this past prosperity can still be glimpsed in the architecture of older homes and the remnants of waterfront structures that recall a time when the bay teemed with oyster boats.

Beyond its oyster-centric past, Higbie offers a distinctive residential character. The streets are lined with a mix of detached houses, offering a suburban feel while maintaining a close connection to the water. The area boasts a tight-knit community known for its strong civic engagement and neighborhood pride. Residents actively participate in local events and initiatives, fostering a sense of belonging and contributing to the preservation of Higbie's unique character. Furthermore, Higbie's appeal is enhanced by its accessibility to recreational opportunities. The waterfront provides opportunities for fishing, boating, and enjoying scenic views of the bay. Parks and green spaces within and adjacent to Higbie offer residents places to relax, exercise, and connect with nature. While not as widely recognized as some of Staten Island's other prominent neighborhoods, Higbie quietly contributes to the borough's rich tapestry with its distinctive history, strong community spirit, and enduring connection to the sea. It serves as a reminder of the island's maritime heritage and the enduring appeal of waterfront living.
