Zermatt, a car-free village nestled in the Swiss Alps, is a world-renowned destination famed for its stunning views of the Matterhorn. The iconic, pyramid-shaped peak dominates the skyline, drawing mountaineers, skiers, and photographers from across the globe. The village itself exudes a charming, traditional atmosphere, with its narrow, winding streets, weathered wooden chalets, and horse-drawn carriages. Despite its popularity, Zermatt has managed to retain a sense of authenticity, carefully preserving its cultural heritage. The absence of cars contributes significantly to this atmosphere, creating a peaceful and clean environment where the sounds of cowbells and rushing streams replace the hum of engines.

Beyond the Matterhorn, Zermatt boasts an extensive network of ski slopes, catering to all skill levels. Linked to the Italian resort of Cervinia, the area offers a vast playground for winter sports enthusiasts, ensuring excellent skiing conditions throughout a long season, thanks to the presence of year-round glacier skiing. In the summer, the mountains transform into a haven for hikers and mountain bikers, with trails winding through verdant meadows, across crystal-clear alpine lakes, and up to breathtaking panoramic viewpoints. The Gornergrat Railway, a historic cogwheel railway, provides access to spectacular views of the Matterhorn and the surrounding peaks, while the Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, accessible by cable car, offers the highest viewing platform in Europe.
