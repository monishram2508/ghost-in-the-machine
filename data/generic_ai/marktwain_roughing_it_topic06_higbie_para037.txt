Higbie's First Point, a secluded beach located within Gateway National Recreation Area in New Jersey, holds a special allure for those seeking respite from the urban clamor. Its remote accessibility, requiring either a lengthy walk along the sandy shoreline from Sandy Hook or a boat, contributes significantly to its pristine and uncrowded character. This very isolation has fostered a unique ecosystem and a haven for diverse wildlife, most notably various migratory bird species. Birdwatchers flock to the area during peak migration seasons, hoping to spot rare terns, plovers, and other shorebirds that utilize the beach as a critical stopover point. Beyond the avian life, the surrounding salt marshes and coastal dunes provide habitat for a variety of small mammals, reptiles, and amphibians, creating a surprisingly rich biodiversity.

The beach itself is characterized by its soft, fine sand and gently sloping shoreline, making it ideal for swimming and sunbathing, especially during the warmer months. The relatively shallow waters extending a considerable distance from the shore offer a sense of safety for families with young children. However, it's crucial to note that Higbie's First Point is an unguarded beach, meaning there are no lifeguards on duty. Swimmers should exercise caution and be aware of potential currents and changing weather conditions. The lack of amenities, such as restrooms and refreshment stands, further emphasizes the need for visitors to be self-sufficient and prepared for a day spent in nature. This lack of development, though inconvenient for some, actively preserves the beach's natural state and contributes to its overall appeal. The views from Higbie's First Point are breathtaking, offering panoramic vistas of the New York City skyline to the north and the vast expanse of the Atlantic Ocean to the east.
