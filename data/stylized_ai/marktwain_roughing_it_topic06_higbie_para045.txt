Higbie, bless his cotton socks, wasn't exactly overburdened with what you'd call "book larnin'." He could barely scribble his own name, but he could tell you the precise moment the sun would hit gold in a particular ravine, or sniff out a promising vein of silver from fifty paces. He possessed a logic all his own, a twisted, backwoods kind of reasoning that somehow, against all odds, usually landed him on the right side of the equation. He was a fella who'd argue that a leaky roof was a blessing because it reminded you to appreciate the sunshine, and then promptly blame you for the mildew growing on your boots.
